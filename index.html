8 Parts of Speech Mad Libs

This mad lib is designed to help you learn the 8 parts of speech.

Descriptions:

Noun is a person, place, or thing.

Pronoun: replace a noun in the sentence to make them shorter and simpler to read. Words like 'I', 'him', and 'yours' are all pronouns. For this mad lib, Possessive Pronouns work the best. They are pronouns that show who owns an object.

Adjectives: describe a noun.

Preposition: link a noun (or pronoun) to other words in a sentence. For this mad lib, avoid the following prepositions because they do not work well in this sentence: among, but, except, of.

Interjections: insert emotion into a sentence. Usually, they are a single word with an exclamation mark after them. Think about someone yelling, 'Yoinks! The goblins are coming.'

Conjunctions: link words or phrases together. 'I will swim today and tomorrow.' For this mad lib, Subordinating Conjunctions work the best. They are linking words that introduce a whole new related phrase.

Verbs are action words. When we talk about them, we usually say the word 'to' before them. 'To run', 'To fish', 'To jump'.

Adverb describe the action. They also usually end in ly like quickly.
